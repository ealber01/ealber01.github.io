Transit

The Challenge:
When there is an uneven passenger distribution among the cars in the subway at peak travel times, certain cars in the subway can become overcrowded and uncomfortable.

The Solution:
The following project aimed to develop an information display system that allowed passengers to (1) position themselves in front of a train car with a lower number of passengers, and (2) have a smoother boarding process and a more comfortable ride.

The Approach:
1. Observational Research: To understand the current problems with public transportation systems, our team conducted field-based observations of local MBTA stations

2. Questionnaires: To understand which individuals our approach should target, we created a questionnaire using Google Forms. In total, we gathered 72 responses.

Initial Designs
Information Display when Entering Station:

Since users needed to see the display information as early as possible, we created an information display that users can view as soon as they entered the station.

Information Display on App:

We felt that users could benefit from an additional mobile application that would allow users to view information about the crowding of each train before even entering the station.

Expert Reviews, Focus Groups, and Usability Testing
After conducting expert reviews, focus groups, and usability testing, we obtained feedback that lead us toward our final design. The main feedback we received was the following: (1) the different alignments and sizes of texts seemed disorganized, (2) the information display had good information, but too much new information for regular users to want to read, and (3) participants felt that the colors on the display were too overwhelming.
