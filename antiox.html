Antiox

Providing health-conscious and health-deprived individuals with access to easy and affordable nutrients that keep the body healthy and the mind stable.

The Problem:
Students at highly rigorous academic institutions have a hard time balancing sleep, eating healthy, exercising, and maintaining their grades.
The Solution:
Antiox is an up and coming startup within the healthcare industry that offers a transdermal patch with vitamins and antioxidants in it. Our product offers a mobile application that makes buying vitamin patches convenient and easily understandable. Our goal is to increase the daily lives of students at academically rigorous and stressful institutions in the Greater Boston Area.
My Role:
As project manager, I was responsible with planning and overseeing all aspects of the project. My expertise in using the user-centered design process allowed me to design a product that was geared toward our target users.

My role as project manager gave me the important experience of learning how to build and maintain a strong team. By overseeing the initiation, planning, execution, and closure of this project, I gained many practical skills that I can later utilize when working on cross-functional teams.

Market

Antiox caters to busy individuals that have have limited time to focus on their health and wellness. The market is further segmented by different industries; specifically, Antiox will focus on college students due to their overwhelming schedules and their shortage of time. Additionally, we want to work with the market that we’re most familiar with, which is why we chose to target college students within the Greater Boston Area.

Market Size

Our market at large consists of the 20.4 million college students attending school in the United States. This number is has grown by 33% since the year 2000, and is expected to keep going as the United States’ economy is increasingly expecting a college degree for more and more positions. Our beachhead market, consisting of college students at academically rigorous institutions in the greater Boston area (Boston University, Harvard University, Boston College, Northeastern University, Tufts University, MIT) is made up of about 119,000 students.
Our Product
A transdermal patch with vitamins and antioxidants and a mobile app to provide our product. After surveying individuals in our target audience, we decided to focus our patches on solving three main issues. The first will be an immune and skin care patch. Our third and final patch will be a hangover patch.

Value

The value that Antiox offers students is the ability to maintain a healthy balance between sleep, physical health, mental health, and academic success. Our patch allows vitamins and antioxidants to diffuse across the skin over just a few hours, much faster than other daily vitamins. Antiox gives students the solution to remain healthy while living their not-so healthy lifestyles.

Designs

We chose to offer our product through a mobile application to create an convenient way for customers in our target audience to purchase our product. Our mobile application will offer the ability for users to subscribe to our blog posts, will keep users updated on latest promotions, and will connect users to our social media pages. The mobile application categorizes our three main patches into easy to understand categories: (1) feeling sick, (2) feeling tired, (3) and feeling hungover. Additionally, rather than only being allowed to purchase one type of each patch at a time, it offers the option for users to customize a pack of patches with a combination of the patches.
